Blog Post: Cisco ACI Data Center Design

The Core – Cisco ACI Fabric
At the center of this topology lies the Cisco ACI fabric, formed by a spine-leaf architecture. Two Nexus 9332C switches serve as spines, and two Nexus 9336-FX2 switches act as leafs. These are interconnected via 40GbE links, ensuring a non-blocking, low-latency fabric. All devices connect only to the leaf layer for scalable, predictable networking.

Policy Control – ACI APIC Cluster
A cluster of APIC controllers connects to the leaf switches to push policies, manage configuration, monitor performance, and enforce security—all through an application-centric model that simplifies operations.

External Connectivity – Management and Outside Networks
A Management Switch connects to the ACI leafs via vPC links, enabling integration with outside networks and seamless VLAN extension for migrations or legacy systems.

Compute Domain – Cisco UCS with Fabric Interconnects
Cisco UCS 6454 Fabric Interconnects link the ACI leafs to compute nodes via high-speed connections, providing end-to-end automation, server policy management, and fabric-based failover for mission-critical SAP workloads.

Storage Domain – NetApp AFF A300
The NetApp AFF A300 connects to the ACI leafs using 40GbE Port-Channels to deliver low-latency access for NFS, iSCSI, or FCoE traffic, with consistent security and QoS policies applied through the fabric.

Blog Post: AI Is Now Growing Faster Than Data Centers Can Handle

Artificial Intelligence (AI) is transforming every aspect of our lives at an unprecedented pace. From healthcare to finance, entertainment to education, AI demand is exploding. But this rapid growth creates a new challenge: data centers—the backbone of our digital world—are struggling to keep up.

  • AI demand is exploding: Businesses and consumers alike are driving a surge in compute needs.
  • Cloud use is growing: Essential for work, play, and everything in between.
  • Data centers are racing to keep up: Capacity isn’t scaling fast enough.

McKinsey says we need to double data center capacity in a quarter of the time it took to build everything so far—an unprecedented construction boom.

Who’s Leading the Charge?

  • Microsoft investing $80 billion in AI infrastructure
  • Google partnering for carbon-aware data centers
  • Oracle using nuclear power for a gigawatt-scale facility
  • Meta with 1.3 million GPUs and huge new builds
  • AWS expanding in Georgia with $11 billion investment
  • And many more: CyrusOne, Equinix, Digital Realty, IBM Cloud, EDGNEX, China Telecom

This isn’t just about “servers in sheds.” It’s about data capacity, power delivery, sustainability, and speed of deployment. By 2030, McKinsey expects $6.7 trillion in new data center investment—70% of it AI-driven. The future of AI depends on solving these challenges now.
